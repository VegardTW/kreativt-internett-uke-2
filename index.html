Apple Lisa 1983

The year was 1982 and Apple was launching a new personal computer: The Apple Lisa. It was affected by its high price, insufficient software, unreliable Apple FileWare floppy disks. Only 10,000 were sold in two years and considered a commercial failure. Despite this Lisa became Apple’s most influential failures. There was a time when a majority of personal computer users interacted with their machines via command-line interfaces. Apple Lisa was the first personal computer with a graphical user interface and a mouse. This technology allowed users to interact directly with their content in a way that a command line never allowed.
